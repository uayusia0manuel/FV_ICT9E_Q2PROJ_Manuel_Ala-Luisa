Puto

Ingredients

  • 1 cup medium grain rice
  • 1/4 cup glutinous rice
  • 1 1/4 cup water to cover rice
  • 3/4 cup + 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp of yeast

Instructions

  1. In a bowl (ideally an earthenware one), mix medium grain rice, sticky rice, and water. Let the mixture sit for 2 days.
  2. Strain the rice, keeping the liquid, then put the rice in a food processor or blender and blend on high speed, gradually adding water one teaspoon at a time. Keep blending, adding 1 tablespoon of sugar, then incorporate the reserved water until it has a pancake batter-like consistency.
  3. When the texture is smooth, return the mixture to the container and then cover it with glad-wrap. Make tiny holes in the glad wrap by piercing it with toothpicks; this will allow the mixture to breathe while fermenting. Put it in a warm location and allow it to ferment for 3 to 4 days. Following 3 or 4 days, the texture of the mixture would become denser.
  4. Carefully incorporate 3/4 cups of sugar into the blend.
  5. Pour into greased puto molds, allow it to sit for 1 hour to rise.
  6. When the mixture is frothy and has expanded, transfer it to a steamer and steam for 15-20 minutes.
  7. Take out of molds and then serve.
