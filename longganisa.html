Longganisa

Ingredients

  • 3/4 kilo ground lean pork pork shoulder or pork butt
  • 1/4 kilo pork fat ground coarsely
  • 4 Tbsp. sugar
  • 2 Tbsp. coarse salt
  • 2 tsp. ground black pepper
  • 2 Tbsp. vinegar
  • 1/4 tsp. salitre
  • 2 Tbsp. chopped garlic
  • 1 Tbsp. rum
  • Atsuete powder dilute in 1 Tbsp warm water (or yellow food coloring)
  • Hog casings

Instructions

  1. Combine all ingredients well in a bowl, excluding the hog casing. Modify the atsuete powder or food dye until you reach the preferred color. Seal the bowl with plastic wrap or a lid and place it in the fridge for 5 days.
  2. Fill the pork casings with the mixture using a funnel or sausage stuffer. If using dried hog casings, immerse them in warm water for 3 minutes prior to stuffing. Cut the longganisa by twisting it into 2-inch sections, then secure with strings.
  3. Leave to air dry for 4 hours in a shaded location. To prepare the longganisa, fry in hot oil until it turns golden brown. Accompany with fried rice and a fried egg.
